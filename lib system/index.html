Dr. Zakir Husain Institute Library - Code of Rules & Regulations

The following rules and regulations have been framed by the Library Advisory Committee of Dr. Zakir Husain Institute to ensure optimal utilization of library materials, resources, and facilities. All registered students, faculty members, and research staff are required to strictly adhere to these official policies.

1. General Code of Conduct

  • A valid ZHI student/employee ID card is strictly mandatory for entry and all circulation transactions. ID cards are non-transferable.
  • Strict silence must be observed inside the reading rooms and study cabins. Cellular phones must be switched off or kept on silent mode.
  • Personal belongings (such as bags, briefcases, folders, and printed books) are not permitted inside the open shelf stack section. These must be deposited at the property counter at the owner's risk.
  • Smoking, drinking, eating, and sleeping are strictly prohibited within all areas of the library premises.

2. Circulation Policies & Overdue Charges

Books are issued to members according to their registration category as outlined in the schedule below. Overdue returns are subject to automatic daily fines.

Membership Category Borrowing Limit Circulation Period Overdue Fine Rate
Undergraduate Students (BCA / BBA) 3 Books 15 Calendar Days ₹2.00 / day per volume
Postgraduate Students (MCA / MBA) 5 Books 30 Calendar Days ₹2.00 / day per volume
Academic Faculty & Research Staff 10 Books 90 Calendar Days ₹5.00 / day per volume

3. Loss & Damage Regulations

  • Reference volumes, encyclopedias, bound university journals, and dissertation thesis projects are strictly restricted for in-library use only and cannot be checked out.
  • Borrowers must inspect books before checkout. Any marking, page folding, underline, defacement, or damage detected on return will hold the last borrower responsible.
  • In case of loss or severe damage to any issued book, the borrower is legally liable to replace the volume with its latest edition or pay twice the current market value of the book.
  • Library clearance certificate (NOC) is mandatory for university examination registration, degree clearance, and institute exit procedures.
